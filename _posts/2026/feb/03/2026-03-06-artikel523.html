⚡ What is leak velocity?

Leak velocity measures how quickly an idea spreads across your audience and beyond. It combines:

  • Speed: How fast do mentions appear after the first leak?
  • Acceleration: Is the rate of new mentions increasing?
  • Reach: How far beyond your immediate audience does it spread?

High velocity = viral potential.

📏 How to measure leak velocity
  1. Set up alerts for key phrases related to the leak
  2. Track timestamps of each mention in a spreadsheet
  3. Calculate rate: mentions per hour over first 24 hours
  4. Note inflection points: When did mentions spike?
  5. Compare to baseline: How does this compare to average leak velocity?
Simple velocity formula:
Velocity = (Mentions in first 24h) / 24

Acceleration = (Mentions hours 12-24) / (Mentions hours 0-12)

🎯 Velocity thresholds for action
  • Velocity > 5 mentions/hour: Monitor closely
  • Velocity > 10 mentions/hour: Prepare content response
  • Velocity > 20 mentions/hour: Act immediately (within 24h)
  • Acceleration > 2x: Viral potential—drop everything

Adjust thresholds based on your audience size.

🛠️ Tools for velocity tracking
  • Google Alerts: Free, basic velocity detection
  • Brandwatch/Talkwalker: Professional velocity analytics
  • Native analytics: Track comment velocity on your posts
  • Custom dashboards: Combine data from multiple sources
  • Manual tracking: Simple spreadsheet for small audiences

⚡ Acting on velocity data

When you spot high velocity:

  1. Acknowledge quickly: "Seeing lots of interest in X—working on something!"
  2. Create fast: Prioritize content on this topic within 24-48 hours
  3. Ride the wave: Promote aggressively while momentum lasts
  4. Capture leakers: Note who contributed early—they're trendsetters

📖 Velocity case study: The 24-hour turnaround

A tech creator noticed a leak about "budget noise-cancelling headphones" had 12 mentions in 3 hours (velocity 4/hour). By hour 6, it was 30 mentions (acceleration 2.5x). He fast-tracked a video, published within 30 hours, and got 800k views—his channel's fastest growth ever.

Key: Velocity data told him this wasn't just one person's idea—it was a wave.

Speed is signal: Leak velocity separates passing thoughts from genuine trends. Track it, and you'll never miss another wave.
