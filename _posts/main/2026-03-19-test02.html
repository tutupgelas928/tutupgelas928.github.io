Leaks are, by nature, timely. A screenshot today is old news tomorrow. But the jobs that those leaks fulfill are timeless. This is the magic of combining JTBD with content creation. By identifying the permanent job behind a temporary leak, you can create evergreen content that continues to attract and help your audience for months or even years. This article shows you how to transform fleeting platform leaks into foundational pieces of your content strategy.

Why Evergreen Content Matters

Evergreen content is the backbone of a sustainable content strategy. While a post about a hot leak might get a surge of traffic for a week, an evergreen post about the underlying problem can drive traffic for years. It builds authority, improves SEO, and provides continuous value to new audience members who are just discovering the problem. For a content creator, balancing timely news with evergreen foundations is key to long-term growth. The JTBD framework helps you build that foundation.

The Job is Permanent, the News is Temporary

Consider a leaked document about a platform's new pricing model. The news itself (the specific numbers, the exact date) is temporary. But the job behind it? That's permanent. The job might be: "Help me choose the right platform for my budget so I don't waste money." This job existed before the leak and will exist after the leak is forgotten. Your goal is to use the leak as a catalyst to create content that serves this permanent job. The leak is the hook; the job is the heart of your evergreen content.

The Transformation Process

Here is a step-by-step process to turn any platform leak into evergreen content:

  1. Extract the Core Job: Ignore the specific details of the leak. Ask: "What fundamental human need does this leak expose?" Is it the need for security, for advantage, for community, for efficiency?
  2. Reframe the Question: Instead of "What does this leak say?", ask "How can I help people solve the underlying problem forever?"
  3. Create a Pillar Content: Write a comprehensive guide that answers the permanent question. This guide should be useful regardless of the specific leak. For the pricing leak example, you might write: "The Ultimate Guide to Choosing a Social Media Platform for Your Budget."
  4. Update with Leaks: When new leaks happen, you don't need to write a whole new article. You can update your pillar content with a new section or a small note, and then promote that update as news.

Example: Turning a Leak into a Pillar Post

Let's walk through a concrete example. A leaked internal email from a social media company reveals that they are deprioritizing link posts in favor of native video.

  • The Leak (Temporary): "Internal memo: Links to see 50% less reach starting next month."
  • The Core Job (Permanent): "Help me make sure my content is seen by my audience, no matter what the platform does."
  • The Evergreen Pillar Post: "The Content Creator's Guide to Platform-Proofing Your Reach: 5 Formats That Always Work."

This pillar post can include a section about video, a section about images, a section about text, etc. It's useful forever. The leak simply provided the impetus and proof for why video is important. You can even mention the leak in the post as a case study, but the post's value doesn't depend on it.

Content Upgrades for Long-Term Value

To make your evergreen content even more valuable, consider adding content upgrades. These are bonus resources that directly help the user get the job done. For our "platform-proofing" guide, upgrades could include:

  • A downloadable checklist for creating platform-friendly content.
  • A video tutorial on repurposing one piece of content into five formats.
  • A template for tracking your content's performance across platforms.

These upgrades are also evergreen and can be offered in exchange for an email sign-up, helping you build a loyal audience. By focusing on the job, you turn a one-time leak into a permanent asset that grows your platform and your authority for years to come.
