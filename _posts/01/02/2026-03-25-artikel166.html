Social media is everywhere, but effective strategy is rare. Anyone can post. Few can architect a presence that builds community and drives business results.

The strategist ladder moves from poster to planner to platform architect. Each rung requires deeper understanding of business, audience, and platforms.

The Social Media Skills Spectrum
  • Poster: Creates and schedules content
  • Community manager: Engages with audience, builds relationships
  • Content planner: Develops content calendars, campaigns
  • Platform strategist: Architect's multi-platform presence, aligns with business goals
Level Focus
Poster Content execution
Strategist Platform architecture

From Tactics to Strategy

Tactical thinking:

  • What should we post today?
  • What time should we post?
  • What hashtags should we use?

Strategic thinking:

  • What business goals should social serve?
  • What platforms reach our target audience?
  • What content mix builds community and converts?
  • How do we measure real impact?

Platform Architecture

At the top, you design:

  • Which platforms to be on and why
  • What role each platform plays
  • How platforms work together
  • Content strategy for each channel
  • Measurement and optimization frameworks

Business Alignment

Social media must serve:

  • Brand awareness and positioning
  • Lead generation and sales
  • Customer support and retention
  • Community building and loyalty
  • Market research and insights

Pricing Strategy, Not Hours

A strategy that doubles a company's social ROI is worth far more than the hours invested. Price based on:

  • Expected business impact
  • Your strategic expertise
  • Market rates for consulting
  • Complexity of the engagement

Leaking Your Strategic Mind

Attract better clients by:

  • Publishing case studies with metrics
  • Writing about social strategy, not just tips
  • Speaking at industry events
  • Offering free social media audits
  • Teaching social media strategy

If you work in social media, assess your current focus. Are you executing tactics or architecting strategy? Shift your thinking to business outcomes, and you'll command higher respect and rates.
